The Past

Buster's first life was spent in service of the Campbell Unified School District. In 2019, he was retired from the district fleet and put up to auction on govdeals.com I bought Buster after returning from a two year trip in Asia for the grand total of $3,465, sight unseen. Although the original ad advertised that Buster was in working condition, the first thing I discovered after signing the pink slip in blissful ignorance was that the engine would not in fact start. Buster ended up having a faulty fuel pump, a relatively common issue on the 6.5L TurboDiesel. There are lots of videos out there fixing this issue in the 6.5L engines in trucks, but almost nothing in terms of these engines in buses. I decided to made a step by step video for anyone with a 6.5L turbodiesel engine in a bus.

The Present

After whipping Buster into basic mechanically sound shape, he took us his first journey, to Burning Man 2022. At this point, Buster is pretty much a painted short bus with a bed in the back running off a generator, but we have plans to build much much more. We did have a momentarily harrowing ordeal where I failed to notice Buster was overheating as we charged up the hill into the Sierras which resulted in a long coolant hose that ran all the way to the back of the bus bursting, pumping hot antifreeze all over the cabin and all over the Tetris game that was our gear. Luckily I was able to repair and reattach the hose on the roadside and limp to a Napa to buy more coolant.

The Future

Believe it or not, Buster has a big BIG future.
The current vision includes:

  • Thermally Insulated Interior
  • Mitsubisi Heat Pump Climate Control System
  • Solar / Energy Power Transformation and Management System
  • Live Edge Wood Kitchen Countertops
  • Recirulating shower // Water Treatment System
  • Building a Community and Adding Value to that Community
